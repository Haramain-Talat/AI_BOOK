# Module 1: Foundations of Physical AI

This module introduces the fundamental concepts of Physical AI, which combines artificial intelligence with physical systems and robotics.

## Learning Outcomes

After studying this module, you should be able to:
- Define Physical AI and distinguish it from traditional AI
- Identify key components of Physical AI systems
- Understand the relationship between Physical AI and robotics

## Introduction

Physical AI represents a paradigm shift in artificial intelligence, where intelligent systems are embodied in physical form and interact directly with the real world. Unlike traditional AI systems that operate primarily in digital environments, Physical AI systems must navigate the complexities of the physical world, including uncertainty, dynamics, and real-time constraints.

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI can be defined as the intersection of artificial intelligence and physical systems. It encompasses:
- Embodied intelligence
- Real-world interaction
- Dynamic environments
- Real-time decision making

## Key Components

Physical AI systems typically include:
1. Sensory systems for perception
2. Actuation systems for interaction
3. Control algorithms for decision making
4. Learning mechanisms for adaptation

## Practical Example

Consider a robot that learns to manipulate objects in a cluttered environment. The Physical AI system must:
- Perceive the environment using sensors
- Plan manipulation actions
- Execute movements with precision
- Adapt to unexpected changes in the environment

## Summary

Physical AI represents a convergence of AI and robotics, creating systems that can intelligently interact with the physical world. Understanding these foundations is crucial for developing advanced robotic systems.
